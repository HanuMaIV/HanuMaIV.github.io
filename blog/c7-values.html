The Yamas and Stereotype Threat

Last week I wrote a blog article on "Sterotype Threat" which you can read here. Claude M. Steele (known for his work on "Stereotype Threat") argues that one of the ways to combat Stereotype Threat is through affirmation. I would like to take this opportunity to explore Patanjali's Yamas from the Yoga Sutra. I feel that they are a helpful guide towards avoiding undesirable behavior.

The Yamas

  • Ahiṃsā (अहिंसा): Nonviolence, non-harm in thoughts, words or actions
  • Satya (सत्य): truthfulness, non-falsehood
  • Asteya (अस्तेय): non-stealing
  • Brahmacharya (ब्रह्मचर्य): celibacy, non-cheating on one's partner
  • Aparigraha (अपरिग्रहः): non-avarice, non-possessiveness

I think the most obviously applicable Yama in dealing with stereotype threat is Ahimsa (non-harm). I feel like I have always generally lived my life with an emphasis on not harming people in actions or words. Sometimes it's hard to not think harmfully on others. We harm ourselves infinitely more when we cause harm to others.

Another concept from the Yoga Sutra that I would like to discuss it "Ishvara Pranidhana". Often people come to me for advice on how to deal with emotional turmoil. I ofter refer them to the concept of Ishvara Pranidhana. (surrender to God). I believe that we are ultimately under the whim of what life throws at us. The thing that can bring peace when things get rough is to just accept it. Find peace in it. You cannot fight that which is unchangeable. Often people confuse this concept with "giving up" but it's more about accepting your current condition without the emotional drama. Without all the drama we are better suited to find clarity on how best to move forward from it.
