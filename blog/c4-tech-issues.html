Superfish and the Problem with most Major Consumer Systems

The Lenovo Superfish security vulnerability was the hot story this week.

For those of you who didn't hear, Lenovo consumer PCs were found to have pre-installed on them software that hijacks your SSL communications. Not only was this exploitation used by Lenovo and Superfish to alter the ads that display in your web browser, but this exploitation left your system wide open to man-in-middle attacks. In these attacks all your "secure" web traffic can be intercepted including bank transactions, site usernames and passwords etc...

Many people were surprised by the implementation by "reputable" companies of this vulnerability, but this situation was a long time coming.

For the past 15 years I have spend days, weeks, maybe even months of my life uninstalling spyware, junkware, adware off of "friends" and family member's computers. Even brand new system installations are bogged down with so much junkware they are rendered practically unusable upon purchase.

The PC industry profit margin is so low that they bundle all kinds of junk into the system. This might bring them more money or the ability to add system "features" to bloat the product label.

The modern windows PC manufacturer is so out of touch with the consumer. There is clearly a demand for streamlined, junk free systems. I feel this is a big part of Apple Computer's success. Which major Windows based manufacturer will follow in their steps first?
