Stereotype Threat

I grew up with my parents being of different races. I definitely had times where I experienced horrible treatment towards myself by my peers and adults. It was very hurtful, alienating and disappointing. I am sure it had an impact on my interest and performance at school. I also believe that it affected my social abilities even more.

We've all heard about the value of a positive mindset in performance situations. The positive mindset can have lasting affects on our performance, work, social life, and even health. So how exactly do stereotypes affect our performance?

Gender, ethnicity, sexual orientation or any labels that divide us as anything other than being a member of the human race can be very dangerous. Research shows that people of equal abilities under-perform when their fears of confirming negative stereotypes are possible.

Take two people of equal mental capacity:

  • Michael: Scored 1500 on SATS, comes from a society where men are told that they are smarter.
  • Jill: Scored 1500 on SATS comes from a society where women are told that they are inferior in intelligence.

According the Stereotype threat research Michael has a higher chance of outperforming Jill on a test where they are told the test is a measure of mental capacity. What is even more interesting to me is that fact that if the test is presented in a way to the test takers that it is not reflective of mental capacity, Jill's chances of scoring equal to Michael no longer remained skewed.

What this potentially shows is that we perform better when we do not feel the risk of proving correct the negative stereotypes that are placed upon us. Stereotypes not just cause division between us but also harm the performance and well being of society as a whole.

We need to start seeing each other as members of the human race and let go of any divisive illusions we may have.
