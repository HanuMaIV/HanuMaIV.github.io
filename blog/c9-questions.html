Asking Good Questions

"There are naive questions, tedious questions, ill-phrased questions, questions put after inadequate self-criticism. But every question is a cry to understand the world. There is no such thing as a dumb question"- Carl Sagan

Carl Sagan beautifully sums up my view on questions in the above quote. He clearly emphasizes the value of any question regardless of it's form of delivery. With that being said let's go over a few techniques to improve your chances of receiving a helpful answer.

Summarize your question in a way that gets to the point.

  • "Earlier today when I was running queries on sqlite, I noticed that I was getting an error. I don't usually get this error. I was wondering if you could help me out with my problem here is my code." <---- bad
  • "Trouble using INNER JOIN in SQLite in Android" <---- good

Provide a general summary of the question before posting any code. In the body of your question, elaborate on your general summary of the question. Describe any challenges you've come across towards understanding the problem. The more you can capture your readers attention within the first few sentences the better your chances are of getting your question answered. Your initial portion of your question will make or break you.

Give enough info in your question to help other people to recreate the problem. Tag your question with the appropriate tags or categories. Be sure to proof read your question before posting. Any mistakes in grammar could potentially make it difficult for the reader to understand what you are trying to communicate. Once you've asked your question be sure to respond to any posts (even if it isn't helpful), this is your way of communicating if the problem has been solved or not.
